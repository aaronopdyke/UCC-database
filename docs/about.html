About this database

This site compiles the unit construction cost (UCC) values embedded in the GEM Global Exposure Model into three lookup lists — by country (ADM0), by first-level administrative region (ADM1), and by building taxonomy — and maps them worldwide.

What UCC means here

UCC is the average replacement cost per square metre of built floor area, in US dollars:

UCC = BLDG_REPL_COST_USD ÷ TOTAL_AREA_SQM

It covers structural and non-structural building components and excludes contents. It is a replacement (reconstruction) cost, not a market or land price. Values come from GEM's national exposure models, which combine census data, national statistics and local expertise; costs are expressed in USD at the model's reference year. The All occupancies value is the area-weighted average across residential, commercial and industrial stock (Σ cost ÷ Σ area), computed by this pipeline.

Each list carries two UCC columns: UCC_USD_PER_SQM in the GEM model's reference-year US dollars, and UCC_ADJ_USD_PER_SQM expressed in the most recent complete year using the World Bank's US consumer-price index (indicator FP.CPI.TOTL). The site displays the adjusted values; the reference year (assumed 2024 — GEM's v2026 release does not pin one) and the applied factor are recorded in data/source_metadata.json.

The three lists

ucc_adm0.csv One row per country × occupancy (RES, COM, IND, TOTAL). 215 countries and territories.
ucc_adm1.csv One row per admin-1 region × occupancy. GEM's ID_1 codes are ISO 3166-2-style.
ucc_taxonomy.csv One row per country × occupancy × GEM taxonomy string × settlement (urban/rural/total).

Each row carries BUILDINGS, TOTAL_AREA_SQM, BLDG_REPL_COST_USD, UCC_USD_PER_SQM and the GEM source commit. In the United States, Canada and US territories, GEM subdivides some taxonomy strings by HAZUS sub-occupancy; those rows are aggregated here (costs and areas summed, UCC re-derived). New Zealand reports non-residential stock as a single NONRES category rather than COM/IND.

Method

A Python pipeline (see the repository) sparse-checkouts the per-country summary files of the GEM exposure model (…), concatenates the 215 countries, recomputes UCC from cost ÷ area (guaranteeing the computed TOTAL rows are consistent), validates against known GEM values, and emits the CSVs plus this site's data files. Every value is traceable to the GEM commit recorded in the GEM_COMMIT column.

Map boundaries

The map uses the World Bank Official Boundaries (CC BY 4.0), downloaded from the World Bank Data Catalog and simplified for the web. GEM admin-1 units are joined to World Bank polygons in layered stages — shared codes (HASC/P-codes), name matching across the World Bank's name variants, then a set of manually reviewed overrides. Where the two sources use different administrative generations, one GEM region may paint several World Bank polygons (France's 2016 regions over the 22 older ones) or one World Bank polygon may carry the area-weighted aggregate of several GEM regions (Finland's pre-2010 provinces, Malawi's regions, single-polygon territories such as Hong Kong) — aggregated polygons say so in their map popup. The World Bank's Non-Determined Legal Status Areas (disputed territories carved out of every country layer) are included as map polygons: where GEM models them they carry those GEM units' values (Jammu and Kashmir, Gilgit-Baltistan, Western Sahara, Golan Heights, Arunachal Pradesh — approximate assignments), otherwise they render as no data. Every join decision is recorded in committed match reports (ADM1, ADM0).

Caveats

  • UCC values are model-level national/regional averages intended for risk assessment at scale — not construction estimating for a specific project or parcel.
  • Costs reflect the GEM model's reference year and USD conversion; check the GEM documentation before comparing across time.
  • Some ADM1 map polygons join GEM units to older World Bank administrative vintages (marked “approximate” in overrides.py).
